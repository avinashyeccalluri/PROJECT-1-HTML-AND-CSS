Glomerulonephritis

The symptoms of Glomerulonephritis includes

It is the inflammation of the tiny filters in your kidneys (glomeruli). Glomeruli remove excess fluid, electrolytes and waste from your bloodstream and pass them into the urine. This disease can develop gradually or acutely. If a person is affected by lupus or he/she is diabetic there are chances that the person might develop the disease. But Glomerulonephritis also has the ability to arise on its own.

  • Pink coloured urine from red blood cells in your urine
  • Foamy urine due to excess protein
  • High blood pressure
  • Fluid retention
  • Swelling in your face, hands, feet and abdomen

Infections

There are certain infections that may be caused by glomerulonephritis. It includes

Post-streptococcal glomerulonephritis : It may develop after the recovery from a throat infection or a skin infection. Our body produces extra antibodies to fight against the disease that can eventually settle in the glomeruli, causing inflammation.

Children are more likely prone to develop post-streptococcal glomerulonephritis than are adults. They also have the tendency to cure earlier.

Bacterial endocarditis: Bacteria occasionally can spread through your bloodstream and lodge in your heart, causing an infection of one or more of your heart valves. If a person is suffering from heart disease then he/she is at a major risk.

Immune diseases

Lupus: It is a chronic inflammatory disease that has the ability to affect skin, joints, kidneys, blood cells, heart and lungs.
