Kidney Donation

Dialysis

Kidney Dialysis

Dialysis is a kind of replacement to the kidney. This treatment helps to do the functions that the healthy kidneys always perform. A doctor would come to this solution only when the kidneys can no more function properly.

Funtions of Dialysis

Removal of the salt , waste and unwanted  acids from the body.

Prevents the extra days or water to stay in body thereby preventing from the building up of the body.

It sees to that the chemicals in the body are maintained at the proper level.

Haemodialysis (HHD)

It is done at least three times a week and lasts for at least four to five hours. It offers flexibility in the timing of the dialysis and how many hours it is to be done. Because there would be no need for the person to go to the clinic. Being in home always does not give the feeling that the person is being treated as a patient which is also a cure for our mental health.

Flexibility, independence and control are the key advantages in the HHD. This is because unlike the satellite haemodialysis or hospital dialysis the person can choose the number of times and the days he wanted the dialysis to be done for him/ her. There would be no mental stress in the home. They will never feel as a patients while undergoing the dialysis in a place where they dwell. So it is highly advantageous than going to the hospital. This ignorance will make them to heal quite quickly.

For our better health conditions the HHD of additional hours is a good solution as it may also help to increase the recovery time.

Peritoneal dialysis

Peritoneal dialysis (PD) is a treatment that makes use the lining of the abdomen called peritoneum. A cleaning solution called dialysate is used to clean the blood. Dialysate absorbs waste and fluid from your blood, using your peritoneum as a filter. Peritoneal dialysis is portable.

It can be done in the home as well. The caution is that the place should be neat and clean. Hence the person’s will have a greater freedom.

Two types:

  • Continuous ambulatory peritoneal dialysis (CAPD) - the day-time bags
  • Automated peritoneal dialysis (APD) -  the overnight machine

CAPD

It is a machine free kind of dialysis. It can be done when the person is involved in the normal works also. So it does not be an obstacle to stop our regular works.

  • Portable type of Dialysis
  • Done everyday through the peritoneal cavity by inserting the bag that contains the peritoneal dialysis fluids.
  • A catheter is generally used for the purpose of letting in the fluids without any change in the path of the flow.
  • The fluid has the ability to attract the water and all the waste from the blood.

The bag has to be changed four times a day. This change is called as the PD cycle. There are two process that is involved in purifying the blood. They are Osmosis and Diffusion.

APD

It can also be called as Continuous Cyclic Peritoneal Dialysis (CCPD). It involves the use of the machine or also called as the cycler unlike the CAPD. In this method a machine is programmed to control how much fluid goes in and out and how often this happens. In case the machine is not working or we have to travel out then we can use manual CAPD bags.

  • On average, five to eight exchanges (cycles) of about two litre’s each go in and out of the peritoneal cavity overnight.
  • The cycle plans when the fluid should be given and when it should be drained out.
  • It is not a portable type

Centre based Dialysis

In this method the dialysis takes place in the hospital. The person has to travel to the hospital to take the treatment. There is another version of haemodialysis called as haemodiafiltration. Generally the person will have three appointments in a week. The hours will vary according to the needs. If you are travelling after the dialysis process then an extra of two hours would be taken to do the treatment along with the general timing of 4-6 hours.

After the dialysis it's in the hands of the patient to take care without leading to further complications.

Transplantation

Kidney Transplantation

In comparison with a lifetime on dialysis, the kidney transplantation is a good treatment of choice for kidney failure. A kidney transplant helps in the chronic kidney disease or end-stage renal disease to help the patient feel better and also to extend his/her life. Advantages of transplantation over dialysis is that

  • It gives better quality of life
  • Lower risk of death
  • Fewer dietary restrictions
  • Lower treatment cost

But for certain people with kidney failure, a kidney transplant may be more risky than dialysis. Conditions that may prevent you from being eligible for a kidney transplant include:

  • Advanced age
  • Severe heart disease
  • Active or recently treated cancer
  • Dementia or poorly controlled mental illness
  • Alcohol or drug abuse

Any other factor that could affect the ability to safely undergo the procedure and take the medications needed after a transplant to prevent organ rejection

Only one donated kidney is needed to replace two failed kidneys, making living-donor kidney transplantation an option.

Transplantation is not a complete cure for a Kidney disease. It is an alternative for dialysis and it offers a longer life span and active function of the kidneys. If the person is medically fit then he can have a kidney transplantation. Usually the kidney is transplanted from a deceased person or a living person. Only after making a thorough check if the donor, the transplantation is done. There are certain medical tests that the donor has to undergo before the kidney transplantation of he/she is a live donor. It includes:

  • Blood tests
  • Ultrasound tests
  • X days of heart and lungs
  • A surgical view of the urinary bladder
  • Checks to know about if the person has developed any antibodies
  • Certain physical tests

Things that a donor should do..

If you want to be a kidney donor then you have to follow certain things in order to be a perfect donor. It includes:

  • Following the dietary restrictions
  • Regular fitness
  • Regular exercises
  • Having an ideal body weight

Procedure of transplantation and aftermath

The operation involved in the transplantation process takes about 3-4 hours. About 2 hours will be needed for its recovery. The kidney will be connected to the bladder for the future functions of kidney. After the transplantation certain kidneys start to function after a few hours in certain cases it might take some time.

After the transplantation there are some medications that a person should follow so that the body accepts the kidney earlier. An important thing that is to be kept in the mind of the acceptor is that he or she should not stop the medications. If it is stopped then the life of the donated kidney may be reduced. So it is good to have a talk with the doctor before the transplantation.

Donation

Kidney Donation

Do you feel that you have to save someone's life? Then Organ donation is the best choice. The organ donation does not make only that person to exist. But it benefits a family. You donate an organ and you make a family to live. This is really the best way to complete your living.

Anyone can be a potential donor Regardless of age or race or status anyone can be a potential donor. But a person's medical history is quite a important consideration in this process. It would ensure that the organ that is transplanted does not create any further problems for both the donor and the acceptor.

Adults can make living donations, meaning that living adults can choose to give an organ, like a kidney, or part of an organ, like a liver, to someone in need.
